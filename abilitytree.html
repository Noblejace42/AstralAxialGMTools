Classless RPG Abilities

Ability Name Move Type Recharge/Type Description
Ace up the Sleeve passive If you would roll to overcome, dazzle, swindle, haggle, or resist peril and you do not add a single Talent die to your dice pool, step up a die in your pool.
Ad Astra Per Aspera light encounter If you would take a wound, you may instead decrease a Talent by 1.
Ask Questions Later passive recovery If you initiate combat with a creature you were meant to interrogate, question, or speak with, you may ask the GM one of the following questions at the end of combat and receive a useful answer: “Why was this creature here?”, “What did this creature want?”, “Why did this creature attack us?”, or “Whom does this creature serve?”
Apothecary passive When you journey planetside you may contest Awareness|Recall; on a success, gain 2 Bandages (I1d6, Special, Consumable 1) or 2 Rations (Special, Consumable 1).
Black Market Connections passive When you arrive at a settlement with a criminal underground, ask the GM: “Who runs the criminal underworld here?”, “What is the main illegal trade?”, “How dangerous is the criminal underworld?”, or “Would I be able to find [item] at the black market here?”
Blot out the Sun heavy encounter Attack using a far-range or long-range weapon then you may expend an ammo to attack again.
Breaker of War medium encounter Attempt to defuse a hostile situation by contesting Presence|Charm. On success, the GM chooses one outcome – from sowing confusion to causing defection – and you increase your Luck when it occurs.
Bulwark medium encounter Declare a creature in your zone; until the encounter’s end, they may add your armor dice to their defense. When they take damage, you may choose to absorb half (this damage cannot be reduced).
Blood for the Blood God light encounter When you kill an enemy with a (Melee), (Brutal), or (Violent) weapon, reduce your Strain by 1.
Bullet Time medium recovery Until your next turn, you cannot be the target of an attack. If you resist peril during this time, step up every die in your dice pool.
Cheat Death passive recovery The next time you would die, instead suffer a Strain break and set your Strain die to 8, then reset your health to its maximum.
Charming and Open light encounter Speak frankly to ask: “Whom do you serve?”, “What do you wish I would do?”, “How can I get you to _____?”, “What are you really feeling right now?”, or “What do you most desire?”—with both parties answering truthfully.
Cheap Shot heavy encounter Attack a creature while stepping up 1 die of your choice. On success, choose two: add 2d6 to damage, gain a surge, move to an adjacent zone, knock the target 10 feet back, or (Melee only) pilfer an unworn/unguarded item.
Companion passive Your companion (Robotic or Organic) has 1d4 in all skills and two bonus traits (choose 2: Crafty, Strong, Fast, Tough; or for robotic, Technical). When nearby and aiding, add 1 die from your companion’s Skills to your pool when contesting or attacking.
Called Shot light encounter When you succeed with a weapon attack (near/long/far range), choose two: instantly kill a creature under 10 health, knock a weapon/object from their hand, inflict a random wound, or regain usage of this ability (increasing your Strain die).
Dark Confidant passive After seeing a contested roll’s results, you may swap a die of the same size between your dice pool and your opponent’s—at the cost of increasing your Strain.
Devilish Po heavy recovery On your next turn, take 3 heavy moves instead of the usual 1 heavy or 2 medium moves.
Discombobulate light encounter Add your Recall and Presence to your next melee damage roll—with no complications allowed on this roll.
Dumb Luck light recovery On a successful peril resistance, gain a surge and regain this ability—or alternatively, increase your Luck by 1.
Engage! engineer engineer encounter Activate an axial instantly, randomly granting 1d4 hardpoint modules on your ship the (Damaged) tag.
Experienced Explorer passive When gained, choose up to 4 terrains (from: Mountain, Desert, Tundra, Wasteland, Jungle, Plains, Forest, Urban, Caves, Asteroid field, Nebula, Ocean) for which you gain a [terrain] expert Talent (d8 expertise).
Forth Eorlingas! passive recovery If you initiate combat while outnumbered, increase your Luck; then choose three different players to respectively increase their Luck, gain a surge, and double their initiative for that combat.
Flash Out heavy encounter If attacking an unsuspecting, surprised, or incapacitated target with an (Explosive) weapon, step up all dice in your pool—and on success, also step up all damage dice.
Flames Of My Desire passive Choose one desire (Pure destruction, Power over others, Conquest, Riches aplenty, Fame and glory, or Esoteric Knowledge). When contesting or attacking in pursuit of that desire, step up both your largest and smallest dice; if the smallest exceeds the largest, a complication may occur.
Focused Training passive When you rest for more than a week, you may decrease up to 2 Skills. For each decrease, increase a Skill by 1.
Font of Knowledge passive When you ask a question or seek knowledge related to one of your Talents, you may have the GM provide a useful or relevant piece of information—in exchange, increase your Strain die.
Fool me Once passive If you fail your second contest or attack in an encounter, you may choose to succeed instead.
Frantic Parry light encounter When about to take damage, you may forgo the damage altogether—but increase your Strain by 1.
Gatecrasher light encounter When contesting Might|Skill to destroy an inanimate obstacle, step up your Might die; on success, choose two benefits (fast execution, no complications, minimal noise, or minimal effort required to fix it).
Give me Liberty! passive recovery If you die defending friends, innocents, or a noble cause, everyone in your party increases their Luck and gains a surge—and your next character may begin with a connection to you (including an item you carried).
Glancing Blow passive You may treat all armor you wear as if it has the (Protective) tag.
Heavy on the Saddle passive Exceed your mass capacity by up to 50. If assigned a “trailblazer” role by a move, you may step up 2 dice in your dice pool.
Hondo’s Return passive When you return triumphantly to civilization, step up your Presence and contest either Presence|Charm or Presence|Might. On success, impress onlookers and gain a bonus die on your next dazzle/swindle/haggle.
Hotshot pilot move pilot move recovery Take two pilot moves on your turn—but increase your Strain by 1.
Herald of Law light encounter Declare a creature as lawbreakers; then, by contesting Presence|Charm, force the GM to choose whether the creature flees, becomes hostile (with a bonus against you), or begs and pleads (gaining a bonus when you next haggle with it).
I Am Vengeance heavy encounter Attack a target in your zone, treating your weapon as if it is (Brutal) and (Violent 3) for this attack.
Indomitable light recovery If you would fail an attempt to overcome, you succeed instead.
Iron-Willed light recovery When your Strain is 10 and would be increased further, reduce your Strain die by 1d4. You may also postpone a Strain break until your next recovery.
Jack Of All Trades passive Whenever you gain a new Talent, you may start it at 1d6 instead of 1d4. (On first gain, also learn a new Talent.)
Journey Before Destination passive When traveling planetside, you may write a new bond with a companion. Reduce your Strain by 1 when journeying with friends you share bonds with.
Juggernaut passive If every worn armor slot is filled with (Heavy) armor or armor with A1d10 or better, step up all your armor dice when you roll defense.
Kingly Wit passive When you dazzle, swindle, or haggle with a creature you share a bond with, step up one die per bond. A complication may grant you an extra bond.
Lucky Tool passive You possess a special tool (describe its type: old and beat up, kept clean, well worn, or uniquely decorated) with a 1d8 die and the (Lucky) tag. When stepping up a die with this tool, step up two dice instead.
Master Quartermaster passive When contesting to set up camp, manage logistics, or cook, step up 2 dice in your pool with no risk of complications.
Melee Master passive Whenever you use a (Melee) weapon, treat it as having (Potent 1). If it already has (Potent X), it becomes (Potent X+1). You cannot be disarmed of your melee weapon so long as you are conscious.
May The Force Be With You passive encounter When dogfighting or opening fire, add your Luck to your dice pool—stepping up one die.
Max Burn passive encounter Expend a fuel to kick your ship's engine into overdrive, doubling your speed modifier until your next turn. (Often used during the merge at the start of combat.)
Port in the Storm passive Whenever you return to a familiar place and are well regarded, choose two: learn how it’s changed, contact a useful NPC, discover a local rumor, or secure an audience with a local ruler.
Punch God medium recovery When defying overwhelming odds or existential terror, step up all dice in your pool to attack or overcome—without incurring complications.
Push beyond limit light encounter Step up 2 dice in your dice pool; if you do, increase your Strain by 1.
Pause Menu passive All weapons gain the (Throwable) tag.
Rampant Hobbyist passive When you Bolster and Carouse, you may forgo the usual benefit to gain a new Talent (1d4).
Rage Against the Machine medium encounter Enter a state of murderous rage – step all dice in your pool for the encounter and, at its end, increase your Strain die by 2.
Resist and Bite light encounter When hit by a melee weapon, choose to take no damage and deal up to half that damage to a target in your zone.
Rigid Focus light encounter When rolling a dice pool that includes a 1d12 Talent, you may reroll up to 2 dice and use the new results.
Rip And Tear heavy encounter Attack a target; if you kill it with your damage, immediately attack another target. Repeat until no further kills occur.
Sage Knight passive After quiet meditation post-battle, decrease your Strain; if it is 0, increase your luck die.
Second Wind: medium recovery Recuperate—restore your ability to fight.
Setup Strike medium encounter Attack an enemy in the same zone as an ally; your ally’s next attack in that zone gains 2 extra dice.
Signature Weapon passive You possess a unique weapon (choose from Ancient Rifle, Ion Handgun, Tactical Shotgun, Carbide Shortblade, or Gyrostabilized Staff) with three selectable special properties (e.g. Accurate, Piercing, Covering, Suppressed, Forceful, Throwable, Cycling). If lost or destroyed, you may recreate it during your next recovery (at half the normal upgrade cost).
Sheer Spite light encounter When rolling your dice pool, roll your Will die and add its value to your total.
Shoot First passive recovery Take the first action in combat—even if surprised.
Steal the Moon passive Gain a “heist planning” Talent at 1d8 expertise. When plotting a heist, add a Skill die to your “heist planning” and, on success, receive an extra useful piece of information from the GM.
Sic Parvis Magna pilot move recovery Increase your advantage by 1d4 and open fire on the starship with the lowest advantage.
Strafe Lurch positioning recovery Move to a zone exactly 3 zones away and immediately attack an enemy there.
Strider passive recovery When journeying or camping, step up all dice in your next roll (which cannot incur complications).
Superior Explorer passive Choose up to 2 terrains (that you lack a [terrain] expert Talent for) and gain a corresponding Talent at d8 expertise; your group then consumes only half the normal rations in those terrains.
Seeing Red passive When dealing damage, you may increase your Strain and add your Strain die to your damage total.
Surging Support passive You may use your surges on other players instead of yourself. Whenever you recuperate, gain a surge—up to 10 surges maximum.
Tactical Reload passive If you spend an hour or more examining your weapon, you may choose to remove one of its tags; then the GM offers two replacement options, one of which you may add to your weapon.
The Ability to Speak passive Regardless of language, you can understand the general meaning of what any intelligent creature is saying.
The Arcane and Esoteric passive When seeking information on a puzzling or mysterious topic, step up your Recall without risking complications.
The Cake Is A Lie passive Whenever you attempt to hack, manipulate, or destroy AI-controlled technology, step up a die in your pool—and then reduce your Strain by 1.
The Great Escape medium encounter Ask the GM one of these questions: “Where is the nearest route to safety?”, “What lies beyond this door?”, “Am I at risk if I stay?”, or “What is the most dangerous thing here?”
Toss A Coin passive When tracking any xenofauna, step up all dice in your dice pool.
Tricks of the Trade passive If you contest and roll maximum on all dice in your pool, increase one Skill by 1.
Troubadour passive When you bolster and carouse, gain credits equal to the result of your roll times 5.
Tough Cookie passive You can accumulate up to 15 Strain before a break occurs; upon recovery, reduce your Strain by 1d4.
Under Pressure light recovery If you would increase your Strain, you may decrease it instead—and add your Strain die to resist peril (without subtracting Strain).
Unbreakable Mind passive Always add your Will die to your Defense pool and any peril resistance attempts.
Vacuum Training light encounter If thrown into the vacuum of space, step up your Fortitude and Will, then contest Fortitude|Will. On success, ignore most adverse vacuum effects and take only half damage.
Volta Gloria passive recovery If you have no surges and initiate combat, gain a surge.
Wall Jumper positioning encounter Move to a non-adjacent zone if there’s a wall (or similar) you can use to climb—no overcoming required.
Wide Wanderer passive If you travel to a system or planet you’ve never visited, step up a die in your dice pool.
Windstance passive When wearing no armor (or only items with the A1d_ prefix), add your Speed and Awareness to your defense.
Would You Kindly? passive recovery Immediately tell if someone is lying or obscuring the truth—and they know you’re onto them. Next time you oppose them, step up one die.
